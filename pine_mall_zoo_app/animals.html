Meet Our Amazing Animals

Discover the incredible diversity of wildlife at Pine City Zoo, home to over 200 species from around the world living in naturalistic habitats.

Elephants

African & Asian

The largest land mammals on Earth, elephants are known for their intelligence and strong social bonds. They have distinctive trunks used for breathing, grasping objects, and lifting water.

Pen E22, next to the amphitheatre

Giraffes

African

The tallest living terrestrial animals, giraffes are known for their extremely long necks and distinctive coat patterns. They feed primarily on acacia leaves at heights other herbivores cannot reach.

G15, just beyond the research centre

Koalas

Australian

These arboreal marsupials are native to Australia and are easily recognizable by their stout, tailless bodies and large heads with fluffy ears. Koalas primarily eat eucalyptus leaves and sleep up to 20 hours a day.

Next to the giraffe enclosure

Dusky Leaf Monkeys

Asian

Also known as spectacled langurs, these primates are native to Malaysia, Burma, and Thailand. They have distinctive facial markings that give them their "spectacled" appearance.

Monkey cage near the Monkey Trail

Giant Pandas

Asian

Native to south central China, giant pandas are easily recognized by their distinctive black and white coloration. Despite belonging to the order Carnivora, their diet consists almost entirely of bamboo.

Panda Canyon

Lions

African

The second-largest big cat after tigers, lions are known for their majestic manes and powerful roars. Once widespread across Africa, Europe, and Asia, they are now primarily found in sub-Saharan Africa with a small population in India.

Northern Frontier

Gemsbok

African

These large antelopes are native to the arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. They are depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia and are known for their impressive horns and striking facial markings.

Just beyond Panda Canyon

Gorillas

African

The largest living primates, gorillas are ground-dwelling herbivores native to the forests of central Africa. Their DNA is highly similar to humans, making them our closest living relatives after chimpanzees and bonobos.

Gorilla cage at GO12

Warthogs

African

These wild pigs are found in open and semi-open habitats in sub-Saharan Africa. They are named for their facial wattles and are known for their distinctive tusks and bristly hair along their backs.

WA11, near the elephant enclosure
